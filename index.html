Experience in Research

Hybrid Structure Modelling

ETH Zurich

Feb. 2021 - July 2021

Intrinsically Disordered Proteins and protein Domains (IDPs/IDDs) posses a relatively flat free energy surface, existing therefore as very dynamical entities. From a computational perspective, the structural characterization of such proteins requires to represent them as conformational ensembles of models. This ensemble description can be later complemented (and refined) by integrating experimental data in the form of restraints over the ensemble of structures. In the course of the project, I worked on the improvement of an algorithm aimed to extract information from Pulsed-Dipolar EPR data, which enables to boost the efficiency of conformational subspace sampling.

Structural Characterization of Proteins

Università degli Studi di Siena & ETH Zurich

Feb. 2019 - July 2019 & Sept. 2020 - Jan. 2021

The atomic resolution structure of various proteins can be obtained with several experimental techniques. During my studies, I mainly worked with X-ray Chrystallography (studying a Human Ferritin during my B.Sc. Thesis) and Magnetic Resonance techniques (especially Solid-State NMR), where I worked on a 5D Automated Projection Spectroscopy method on a 850 MHz magnet.

Education

ETH Zurich

M.Sc. in Chemistry

Sept. 2019 - Aug. 2021

In the Chemistry department (D-CHAB) of ETH Zurich, I got interested on structural description of proteins through Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopy (NMR and EPR). In particular, I mainly focused on the study of a subclass of proteins called Intrinsically Disordered Proteins (IDPs). These have been very popular since the early 2000s, given that they have reshaped the structure-function paradigm. In fact, due to the lack of a well-defined 3D structure, they are still eluding a precise structural characterization, which is fundamental to understand the involvment of these proteins in neurodegenerative deseases.
